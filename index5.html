About Dr. Kondappan

Researcher in Quantum Mechanics and AI-driven Lab development and automation, with a passion for advancing quantum technology and theoretical physics.

Research Background

Dr. Kondappan is a scientist with extensive experience in experimental quantum optics, quantum computing, and AI-driven STEM labs development. His work experiences were focused on building magneto-optical traps, preparing single atoms in optical tweezers, developing atom chips, and designing systems for quantum computation and entanglement measurements. He has expertise in optical component prototyping, light-matter interactions, and algorithmic architectures for lab development, automation, and overall building.

Education

  • Doctorate in Physics (2018-2024), New York University Shanghai and East China Normal University, Shanghai. Dissertation: "Quantum non-demolition-based entanglement of cold atoms on atom chip."
  • Master of Philosophy in Physics (2013-2014), University of Madras. Dissertation: "Qubit-Oscillator System: Analytical studies in ultra-strong coupling regime."
  • Master of Science in Physics (2010-2012), Bharathidasan University. Project: "Wireless-quantum information processing experiment based on Swartz-Hora."

Experimental Quantum Physicist with 10+ years of laboratory experience in AMO Physics, Qubit architecture, and quantum hardware design (Single Atom, BEC qubits), Atom Chip, Dipole Traps, general and ML algorithms, and a PhD in Physics from NYU-ECNU. Previously at Raman Research Institute (RRI).
